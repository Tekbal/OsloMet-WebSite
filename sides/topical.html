INTERNET ADDICTION AND ITS EFFECTS ON POLITICS
Internet addiction is increasing every day. One of the most concrete examples of this is that in research conducted in countries where the internet is used extensively, the young population spends an average of five hours a day at the screen.1 Due to the desire of people to be liked on social media platforms, millions of shares are made daily, which reveals internet addiction. In addition, a new term defined by scientists as nomophobia entered our lives in 2008. Nomophobia (No mobile phobia); it means excessive fear of disconnecting from the communication provided by the mobile phone.2 We can cite the Arab Spring as an example of the impact of the spread of the internet on politics in a way that creates independence in our day. The Arab Spring is a regional and social movement that arose because of the demands of the Arab peoples for democracy, freedom and human rights. Protesters organized using social media platforms such as Twitter and Facebook, protests, rallies, demonstrations, and experienced civil conflicts, and as a result dictator toppled governments in some countries and in some countries has been forced to resign. The Arab spring draws attention to the fact that people cannot take to the streets in repressive regimes because they are afraid of being punished, and the internet has become an important mission in these countries by turning into a means of protest. 3 When we look at today's developments, we can predict that people's internet addiction and the effect of the internet on politics will increase. While politics and social media are intertwined in developed countries, oppressive countries (eg North Korea, China, Iran, etc.) restrict the use of social media because they are worried about mass protests.
PHONE SOVEREIGNTY
In today's world, it has become a fact known to everyone that smartphones make our lives easier. But over time, this has made people's phones feel like a limb of their own, and it is happening. We can now say very comfortably that mobile devices have reached the level of addiction. Research reports that people, especially young people, spend about 1/51 of a day on the phone. The natural consequence of this is a waste of time and inefficiency. And it strikes the biggest blow to relations between people. It formed the instinct of making easy money. While accelerating the access to material values, it accelerated the need to lose them to that extent. Because of the phones that are full of social media programs that fuel people's feeling of being liked, individuals are forced to record every moment and share them. As a result, some people end their day without talking to anyone and have intense psychological problems. In addition to the negative effects of mobile phones, we should not forget about their numerous benefits. Thanks to phone banking allows us to solve this very difficult task in less than a minute. 20th century, the camera and the film industry had a huge impact on society. But with the spread of phones, now everyone has become an actor and director. Millions of data are recorded per minute, and most of them take their place on devices as memories. It is a fact that we cannot finish counting the negative sides and positive sides of phones. But we agree that both of them have a negative side to us. We must not miss a single moment in our short life. We need to stop spending our free time with phones and computers.
